Nutrient contents of food

This dashboard visualizes the nutrient contents of about 400 products, for 16 different nutrients, measured in 9 different countries.

The data is organized as a hierarchy of categories (categories, subcategories and subsubcategories), with products inside the subsubcategories. This hierarchy is visualized as a circle packing in the bottom left quadrant and you can click on these circles to zoom in on the different categories.

Depending on the current focus, the radar plot in the top right quadrant shows the average nutrient contents in every sub-unit, normalized with the Recommended Daily Intake (the white circle). Try scrolling, and hovering over the axis labels and the colored lines.

You can also navigate by using the dropdown menus in this sidebar. The buttons in the sidebar can be used to move a different plot to the large bottom left quadrant, in order to get a better look at it.

The barchart on top shows the amount of nutrients for each of the seven countries. The bars update depending on the chosen categories, country and/or food items. Each nutrient on the x-axis has it's own graph with a separate scale to make the bars visible regardless of highly varying nutrient values.
